How to Manage Client Collections with a Google Sheets Accounts Receivable Tracker

Is your business suffering from outstanding invoices?

For small business owners, freelancers, and agency operators, cash flow is the lifeblood of your company. You can have thousands of dollars in booked revenue, but if that money is sitting in unpaid client invoices, you won’t be able to pay your business expenses, cover your software costs, or pay yourself.

This is why managing "Accounts Receivable" (the money your clients owe you) is so critical.

Yet, many service providers manage their outstanding invoices using memory or scattered email threads. This lack of structure leads to forgotten billings, delayed collections, and lost revenue.

The solution is to maintain a dedicated google sheets accounts receivable tracker.

In this guide, we will show you how to build a collections tracker inside Google Sheets and share a simple, polite email script to follow up on late payments.

What is an Accounts Receivable Tracker?

An Accounts Receivable (AR) tracker is a centralized dashboard that lists every invoice you have issued that has not yet been paid. It tracks four essential pieces of data:

  • The Client Name and contact details.
  • The Invoice Number and total amount.
  • The Issue Date and Payment Terms (e.g., Net 14 or Net 30).
  • The Days Outstanding: How many days have passed since the invoice was issued.

By monitoring your "Aging Invoices" (invoices grouped by 0-14 days, 15-30 days, and 30+ days overdue), you know exactly which clients need a follow-up email.

How to Follow Up on Overdue Invoices (The Polite Persistence Method)

Chasing clients for money is uncomfortable. To make the process stress-free and professional, use this simple, automated three-step follow-up system:

Step 1: The Day of Due Date (A Friendly Reminder)

Send a short, friendly email on the morning the invoice is due.

  • Subject: Friendly reminder: Invoice [Number] is due today
  • Script: "Hi [Name], I hope you’re having a great week! Just a friendly reminder that invoice [Number] for [Project Name] is due today. You can find the PDF invoice and bank details attached. Let me know if you have any questions!"

Step 2: 7 Days Overdue (The Firm Follow-Up)

If the invoice remains unpaid after a week, send a firmer reminder.

  • Subject: Action required: Invoice [Number] is 7 days overdue
  • Script: "Hi [Name], I am writing to follow up on invoice [Number], which is now 7 days past due. Could you please let me know when we can expect payment? Thank you!"

Step 3: 14 Days Overdue (The Direct Phone Call)

If two weeks pass, stop emailing. Pick up the phone and call your client directly. A polite conversation resolves payment delays much faster than an email thread.
